Design principles

Six decisions that shape the optimized process.

01
Minimum viable process
Concept Development and Business Case (once separate stages) are merged. Build, Launch Prep, and Launch are unified into one delivery phase with a readiness checkpoint before go-live.
02
Both gates are non-negotiable
Filtering ideas before business case investment and before build investment delivers the highest ROI of any process element. Both gates are retained and strengthened.
03
Governance from Policy v1.0
The Idea Governance Advisory Committee — CFO-chaired, ~55% external votes, conflict-of-interest rules, quarterly monitoring role — is adopted wholesale from the v1.0 policy.
04
Implementation depth from the Guide
Agile/Scrum, the 24hr/48hr escalation chain, three complexity tiers (Complex/Medium/Simple), and the formal launch readiness confirmation are all carried forward.
05
KPIs at every phase
Process A had no per-stage metrics. The optimized process defines measurable outcomes at every step, making performance visible and improvement actionable.
06
Continuous loop, not a project
Monitor & Improve feeds validated market insights, customer needs, and competitive intelligence directly back into Strategic Ideation — making innovation an operating discipline.

What changed and why

Traceability from source processes to the optimized design.

Element In the optimized process Source Rationale
Process steps 6 (4 stages + 2 gates) Redesigned Reduces 14-step and 7-step processes by merging sequential phases with the same owner and output type
Governance committee Idea Governance Advisory Committee — CFO chair, 9+ members, ~55% external, conflict-of-interest rules Policy v1.0 Process A had no formal committee. External members bring market perspective and reduce internal bias
Idea Qualification Criteria Weighted scoring matrix, min. 55% threshold Guide Policy v1.0 Combines Guide's 6-criteria weighted matrix with Policy's minimum threshold and written feedback requirement
MVE / rapid prototyping Introduced at Ideation stage — before committee submission Guide Policy v1.0 had no experimentation mechanism. Running lightweight MVEs before Gate 1 reduces the cost of rejection
Business case depth TAM/SAM/SOM, P&L, cash flow, payback period, competitive landscape, risk register Policy v1.0 Policy v1.0's business case framework is the most rigorous and is adopted in full
Complexity tiers Complex / Medium / Simple — scales Agile methodology, testing depth, and documentation requirements Guide Policy v1.0 applied a single process to all ideas regardless of size — creating unnecessary overhead for simple initiatives
Escalation process 24hr resolution → 48hr PM escalation → Investment Committee for strategic issues Guide Policy v1.0 mentioned escalation without defining it. Clear SLAs prevent issues from stalling delivery
KPIs per phase Defined measurable outcomes at every stage and gate Policy v1.0 Process A had no per-stage metrics. Without measurement, improvement is guesswork
Continuous loop Monitor & Improve feeds insights back into Strategic Ideation Policy v1.0 Process A was purely linear. The feedback loop transforms innovation from a project into a sustained capability
Product retirement Included within Monitor & Improve — data migration, user communication, sunset timeline Guide Policy v1.0 had no retirement path. Products without a planned sunset accumulate technical debt and support cost
